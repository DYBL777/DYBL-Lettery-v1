Most people start with: "AI, build me a lottery."

I started with: "How can I build the biggest, fairest, most transparent, user-friendly game in the world?"

That's the difference. That's everything.

The first question to AI came quickly after. Once the direction was clear.

That's a BUILD question. Not a THINK question.

I already knew WHAT. AI helped with HOW.

## Mistakes Are Essential

Most people don't start because mistakes terrify them.

So they never find out if it works.

Here's the truth: mistakes are the lifeblood of great things. Every wrong turn teaches. Every bad question leads to a better one. Every failure narrows the path to success.

The raw files show 600 hours of my mistakes. Every wrong turn. Every bad question. Every time I went in circles.

And it still worked.

No CS degree. No funding. No team. Humble background. Poor in money (not in the things that mattered). No formal education.

Built a DeFi primitive anyway.

If you're waiting until you're ready, you'll wait forever. Start. Fail. Learn. Repeat.

## How Questions Work

I've trained my mind to work a certain way. Still learning after 30 years.

Ask a "how" question. A real one. To yourself. Then let it sit.

Don't force. Don't grind. Flow. Allow.

The answer might come while feeding a seagull. Or at 3am. Or in the shower. Doesn't matter where. It comes.

This sounds soft. It isn't. It's a discipline. Hard to learn. Not for everyone.

But it's teachable. And it works.

## Step By Step

I didn't see the whole vision first and then build it.

I solved one problem at a time. The next question wouldn't come until the previous problem was fixed.

First: Do the numbers even work? Hours of playing with Aave rates, user spend, ticket prices. Making sure this wasn't fantasy.

The numbers worked.

Then: "How can I get around 6/49 regulatory issues?"

Couldn't just copy existing formats. Needed something different.

Within an hour or two, the answer came: Lettery. A meme alphabet. A different game entirely.

---

## How Lettery Found Its Alphabet

A mini-process inside the bigger process. This is exactly how it works:

Started with 26 characters. A-Z. Simple alphabet.

Problem: Odds too short for treasury to work.

New question: "How do I make odds better for treasury AND better for users?"

Tried 33 characters. Nice number. Added some extras.

Still too short.

Then: 42 characters. A-Z plus 0-9 plus specials.

Perfect. Better odds than 6/49. More fun to play. Treasury works.

That took a few hours. Failed twice. Adjusted twice. Then it clicked.

Then: "What do I call it?"

"It's a lottery with letters" came into my head. Two seconds later: LETTERY.

So obvious. That's how it works when the thinking is clear.

Only AFTER Lettery was solved did the next big question arrive.

---

## The 24-Hour Question

Then Pavlov - the behavioural toggle. Then Legacy mode. Each one a problem solved before the next question arrived.

Then the big one.

"How can I make the jackpot bigger?"

This one wouldn't leave. 24 hours, circling. Not forcing. Just... holding it.

The number one reason people play the lottery is jackpot size. I knew that. But how do you make it bigger without more players?

First came playing with the numbers. Aave rates. User averages. Trying to find more yield.

Then it popped:

"What if I retain 5% of the prize pot forever and never pay it out?"

Then: "Why not 10%?"

Then: "What if it just keeps rolling? Forever?"

The Eternal Seed.

It compounds. Alone. Doesn't even need Aave yield - that's just bonus. The seed grows by itself because it never leaves.

The floor can only rise.

## How To NOT Prompt

The 600 hours of dialogue isn't a prompt library. It's the opposite.

It's how to NOT prompt. How to converse. How to argue. How to catch AI when it's wrong. How to lead the thinking while AI does the building.

Most AI education teaches you to prompt better. This teaches you to think better - then let AI follow.

## The Honest Truth

The raw files aren't examples of perfect prompting. They're the opposite.

You'll see where I could have done it faster. Where I asked badly. Where I got emotional. Where I went in circles.

That's the point.

It's not how an expert uses AI. It's how a real human thinks WITH AI. Messy. Emotional. Inefficient sometimes. Tired.

From one question to myself, a system:

The flagship demo. Meme alphabet. 42 characters. A lottery where the jackpot has a rising floor.

**The Overflow**
What happens when Lettery succeeds. Expansion into new pots. One lottery becomes infrastructure.

**The Guardians**
Trillion-dollar decisions need stewardship. A governance model for expansion.

**The Resilience Framework**
8 failure scenarios. What breaks, what we'd do, what we honestly don't know yet.

**Founder Succession**
A taper from founder share to zero over decades. The founder eventually returns to the Seed.
